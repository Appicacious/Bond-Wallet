Bond Wallet Rules

PRIZE BOND

  • A prize bond can be of any of the following denominations:
    Rs. 100, Rs. 200, Rs. 750, Rs. 1,500, Rs. 7,500, Rs. 15,000, Rs. 25,000, Rs. 40,000 – Premium

  • Every valid prize bond has to have a number, a prefix and an issue date:

    • Prefix: It is up to 2 characters long and is always an alphabet

    • Number: It is always 6 characters long and contains only digits from 0 ~ 9

    • Issue date: It is contained in the stamp that is affixed on the prize bond when it is issued by the SBP. A prize bond without such a stamp is not considered valid.

    • Bond wallet assigns 3 more fields to a prize bond which are as follows:

      • Creation date: Not visible to the user. It is the date (and time) when a prize bond is saved by the user in Bond Wallet

      • Purchase date: It is the date when the user purchased the prize bond

      • Collection: A prize bond can be saved in Bond Wallet only with reference to a Collection. A collection is a virtual grouping which may contain any number of prize bonds of different denominations. It is up to the user to think of a good use of this field.

        • User may choose to have just one collection and save all their prize bonds in it.

        • It may be used to record family member name in case different members have different prize bonds and they want to record all of them in just one instance of Bond Wallet.

        • It may be the year grouping all prize bonds purchased in one year in one collection.

RULES:

  • Prefix and Issue date are not mandatory fields. However, if any one of these is entered by the user, the other one is required too. It is the app’s way of saying that either the user enters complete details or not.

  • Issue date and purchase dates can’t be future dates.

  • Issue date would always be earlier than or same as purchase date, for obvious reasons.

  • Purchase date is not mandatory to be entered by the user but the app always records current date as purchase date. User can change it at the time of saving the bond or even afterwards but she is not allowed to delete it.

  • There can’t be two collections of the same name.

  • Duplication:

    • There can be numerous prize bonds with the same number in the market but each such prize bond would have a different prefix and issue date.
      Probably, now you can guess why Bond Wallet allows either none of the prefix and issue date or both.

    • A bond is considered duplicate when the combination of prefix, bond number and issue date are the same. Saving duplicate bonds is not allowed by Bond Wallet.

    • Duplication is checked across all collections. In other words, a bond having same prefix-number-issue date can’t be saved in Bond Wallet even if the collection name is different.

    • Draw results, when published by the SBP, contain bond numbers only, not the prefix or issue date.
      So, what happens to the bonds with same number if that number is in the draw results? Each instance of such prize bond is entitled to claim the prize. For example, if there are 20 prize bonds in the market with the same number 122011 (with different prefix and issue date, of course) and this number gets a second prize in a draw, all 20 will be entitled to claim the prize.

    • This point is not about duplication but it somehow is relevant to be mentioned here. A bond with the same prefix-number-issue date combo can win a prize more than once in its lifetime. It is only possible if at the time of claiming of prize, the prize bond is not retained by SBP.
      As per my current information, this practice has now been discontinued by SBP for every denomination. However, old prize bonds are still in market that have won a prize in the past and were not retained by SBP at the time of claiming of prize. Such prize bonds are still entitled to win prizes.

    • Bond wallet fully caters to all the above-mentioned scenarios so that if in future, SBP again starts reissuing the same prize bond after payment of prize, the app logic is not required to be modified.

FEATURES:

  • At the time of saving a bond, denominations are made available in the form of a list. User can pick and choose.

  • Collections, if any, are available in the form of a list. However, user can simply type in a new collection and it will be automatically created at the time of saving the prize bond. User doesn’t need to create a new collection on another screen first and then come back to complete the Add prize bond form.

  • Issue date and purchase date can be picked from a date picker widget only. User is not allowed to ‘type’ a date. This provides ease and data security at the same time.

  • Save button is intelligent enough to enable or disable itself as the user enters data. It is enabled only when all the data is valid; otherwise it would be disabled and some error message would be appearing requiring user to make the correction.

  • Prize bond series are automatically generated for the user. She has to just provide the first and the last number of the series. However, series containing more than 100 prize bonds are not allowed.

  • Prize bond series can be manually edited before saving. User can benefit from this feature for dropping those bond numbers that he doesn’t possess in a series.
    For example, if the user has an incomplete bond series where she has, let’s say, 82 bonds out of 100. So, instead of punching those 82 bonds one by one, she can provide first and last number of 100-bond series and then manually edit it to drop those 18 bonds that she doesn’t possess, thereby saving a lot of precious time.

  • Bond wallet provides real time validation of prize bond data when the user attempts to save a new prize bond or a series thereof. No other prize bond app on Play Store is offering such a feature.

    • Prize bond or series is checked against all draw results available in the app and all existing prize bonds saved in the app, on real-time-basis (as the user punches data) and findings are shown in an info window which is placed right beneath the bond number widget.

    • If the entered prize bond is found in another collection, it is reported as a duplicate bond and user is not allowed to save it.

    • If the entered prize bond series has let’s say, 5 such duplicate bonds, it would be reported as such in the info window; however, that doesn’t block the user from saving the series. It is just that those 5 bonds won’t be re-saved.

    • If the entered bond has won a prize in any of the past draws offered by Bond Wallet, this fact would be reported. By default, such a prize would be ignored unless the user explicitly accepts the prize.
      So, if a user is entering a bond which has actually won a prize, she would just save the bond and accept the prize both at the time of saving the prize bond instead of two different entries.
      Another benefit is that the user gets to know of any such winning that she erroneously overlooked before the use of this app.

    • It took me almost a month in total to perfect it and the best thing is that it takes less than 2 seconds to perform all the above validations on even slower devices. On faster one, it takes less than a sec.

PRIZES

  • Different denominations offer different number of prizes with different amounts but there are always 3 categories: First prize, Second prize & Third prize.

  • A bond can win if it qualifies on the following:

    1. The bond number appears in a relevant draw

    2. The issue date of that bond is 60 days prior to the draw date

    3. The draw in which it appears is not older than 6 years.

    4. There are other conditions like the physical condition of the bond and whether it is fake or not but these are beyond the scope of Bond Wallet

  • Bond wallet takes care of all the above conditions (1-3) and performs checking in following two ways:

    • Automatic checking:

      • A prize bond or series is/are checked automatically against all valid available draws (valid = not older than 6 years) right at the time such prize bond or series are saved.

      • Winners are declared after taking into consideration the issue date of the prize bond such that prize bonds have issue date not older than 60 days from the draw date are not declared as winners.

      • If any of the prize bonds have won prize, such fact is shown in the Info widget right inside the Add Prize bond screen.

      • Declared prizes are ignored by default but offers an edit button to either Accept / Ignore the prizes listed therein.

      • Prizes are ignored by default due to the fact that if a user is entering prize bonds in Bond Wallet for the first time, there is more chances that they have claimed prizes against these bonds already.

Secondly, since same numbers can appear in multiple draws, user’s newly purchased bonds can appear as winners especially when the user doesn’t mention prefix and issue date. So, in most of the cases, user would just review the prizes and let them be ignored.

However, to cover the rather less probable case where the bond actually won a prize, there is that option to Accept the prize. For that, the user would open the edit screen and tap on the prize icon to Accept it as a winning prize bond.

      • A prize once ignored would never be reported as a prize whenever the user checks such bond even against old draws. This is done to save the user of the pain of ignoring the same prizes again and again.

      • Home page offers a Prize button/card that shows the list of prizes (collection and denomination wise). Such prize list shows prizes divided into two main categories (tabs), i.e., Accepted and Ignored and you already know from above description what sort of prizes these two categories host.

      • Ignored prizes are shown as dimmed in order to make them even appear as ignored. However, users have the option to re-accept any of these, if they later find out that ignoring the prize was a mistake on their part. Re-accepting would shift such an ignored prize to the Accepted tab/category.

      • Accepted prizes are actually unclaimed prizes and they stay as unclaimed and reported as such until these are marked as claimed by the user. Collections page shows the number of unclaimed prizes in red in each denomination block that blinks for some time, each time the user opens that page, so that the user remembers there is something pending to be done on his part. An accepted prize can later be ignored if the user finds out that accepting the prize was a mistake on her part. Ignoring at this stage will shift this prize from Accepted to Ignored tab.
        Treatment/presentation of unclaimed bonds can still be improved and therefore, I would like some suggestions from a user’s perspective.

      • When the user attempts to mark an accepted prize as claimed, he is prompted with a question about whether this prize bond was surrendered to SBP at the time of claim of prize or not. As per my discussion with a banker, now all prize bonds are surrendered but I would still like to have a definitive word on it. Till then, this prompt stays.
        Treatment in both cases is as follows and there is room for discussion too:

        • Not-surrendered: Such prize bonds keep on appearing in the prize bond list and can be further checked in future or past draws because these are still eligible for a prize win.
          Such prizes are listed in Accepted tab and marked as claimed with the prize amount crossed.

        • Surrendered: Such prize bonds quit appearing in the prize bond list because these are not physically available with the user anymore and these are not eligible for further wins.

However, their prize will keep on appearing in Prize list under Accepted tab. It will be marked as claimed with both the prize amount and bond number crossed. Reason behind such decision was that the user should be able to know about the history of her earnings even if the prize bonds are surrendered after claiming of prizes.

    • Manual checking:

      • For saved prize bonds:

        • Any saved prize bond can be checked against a single, multiple of all draws. However, normally there won’t be any reported prizes because all prizes would have been reported at the time of saving the bonds and either ignored or accepted right at that time.
          However, now after the suggestion of Babar sb, I may implement this screen such that earlier Accepted (Both unclaimed and claimed) prizes also appear here.

        • The best part of Bond Wallet is that it offers to check all prize bonds of a denomination saved in a collection be checked against any number of valid draws without the hassle of picking them one by one and get the prizes, if any. This is a paid feature and paid users are expected to utilize this as soon as a draw is released. However, as the heading suggests, this is a manual procedure and while the users will receive the notification about a new draw and while it will be downloaded automatically, the user would still have to perform checking her prize bonds against that draws manually.

      • Unsaved prize bonds:

        • There is no restriction to first save a prize bond and then check draws. Random checking of prize bonds is also available in the following two ways both of which are made available right on the Home screen:

          • Bond wallet offers a quick search option where the user selects a denomination, pick one, many or all draws relevant to this denomination and provide a bond number or a bond series to search these withing these draws. The prizes, if any, are listed on next screen.
            If a single draw was selected to check the bonds, draw number and date will be shown in the header. If multiple or all draws were selected, draw info will be shown with every prize. Prize info like level of prize and prize amount will also be shown with each.

            • Free users are required to enter all 6 digits of a single prize bond to perform a search. Further, they are not allowed to search a prize bond series.

            • Paid users can enter 3 digits and all prizes will be presented to the user in which these 3 digits appear. Suggestions are welcome on whether prizes where the given digits are the starting digits are presented or whether to leave it as currently implemented.

They can also give a range of bond number by mentioning first and last number. So far, such a range has been capped at 1,000 bonds….may be increased or decreased.

          • Bond wallet offers an option to view the complete draw list in a contemporary way. In my opinion, it wasn’t necessary but I’ve noticed user who still prefer this approach…the more hassle, the more satisfaction approach. There they will have to scroll through the list to find a bond number. I would have provided the search option here but that what the Quick search feature described above already offers.

  • Summary: prizes go through the following status changes:

    • Ignored: These prizes are not relevant to the user’s prize bond because the prize was already claimed by someone else against the same prize bond OR the user didn’t bother to enter the issue date at the time of saving the prize and now all past prizes against the same number (without consideration of prefix/issue date) are popping up. These need to be ignored once and for all so that these don’t appear again as prizes.

    • Accepted / Unclaimed: These are the type of prizes that user’s prize bond has actually earned. If user provided a prefix and issue date at the time of saving the prize bond, then Bond wallet is 100% sure about the winning eligibility of that prize bonds; if issue date was not provided, then we would have to rely on the user’s feedback. These prizes are expected to be claimed by the user from SBP and there they can be either surrendered to the bank or retained by the user.

  • Usage restriction: Bond wallet imposes a usage restriction on free users to save 15 prize bonds in total. Beyond that they will either subscribe in order to avail unlimited storage or keep on checking their bonds against draws without the option to record a prize and track its different statuses.

DRAW RESULTS

  • Draw results are pushed to Bond wallet online after these are released on SBP website. This is a manual process from developer point of view and requires only 5 to 10 min to format and upload the results. From user’s point of view, there is nothing manual about it as explained below.

  • This requires a working internet connection, for obvious reasons.

  • Each user gets a notification whenever a new draw result is made available.

  • User doesn’t need to manually download new draw results. Bond wallet receives these and saves these in permanent storage automatically.

  • Once downloaded, all results can be accessed offline.

  • However, an option is still provided where the user can check for updated draws manually. This is not really necessary but has been provided just in case.

BACKUP / RESTORE

  • Bond wallet allows local back up of the following stored data:

    • Collections

    • Prize bonds

    • Prizes earned

  • User has the option to select the location where she wants to save the backup file.

  • Backup file stays on the device even after the app is uninstalled. However, it would be removed if the user resets her device. In such cases, user can easily pull the file and store it elsewhere before resetting her device

  • When the backed up data is restored, it replaces the existing data with the backed up data. User is properly warned about this fact before she plans to initiate a restoration process.

USAGE RESTRICTIONS / PREMIUM FEATURES:

  1. Bond wallet is free to download and use but a free user has restricted access to app storage and premium features.

  2. A free user can save up to 1,500 prize bonds and 15 prizes in up to 2 collections. A paid subscriber has unlimited storage access.

  3. Bulk lookup: A free user has access to all draw results just like a paid subscriber but a free user can look up a single bond while paid subscriber can look up all bonds of a denomination at the same time.
    For example, a paid subscriber has 1,000 bonds of Rs. 200 in a collection and a new draw is released. She can just select that draw and all the 2,000 bonds of the that collection would be looked up in that draw in kind of a single click and winning bonds reported to the user in a jiffy. Free user is allowed access to draw results but she will have to look up one bond at a time, thereby making this feature valuable and tempting enough to go for a paid subscription.

  4. All types of users can back up their saved prize bond data but only paid subscribers are allowed to restore backed up data.

  5. Avoidance of abuse:

    • Consider a scenario where a user purchases paid subscription of let’s say, one month. In that month, she punches let’s say 5,000 prize bonds of different denominations in let’s say 5 different collections. Further she cancels the subscription before the completion of one month. Such a user has overcome the usage restriction of 1,500 bonds and 2 collections but still she isn’t allowed to use the premium feature of bulk lookup mentioned at 3 above. Whenever a new draw is released, she would have to go through each of the many bonds of that denomination one by one in order to see if any of these won a prize or not. This keeps the paid subscription still tempting for her.

    • Taking above scenario a step further, if she purchases more bonds, she can still manipulate her storage of 5,000 bonds by editing prize bond data and punching data of new bonds instead if the upcoming draw is of newly purchased bonds. This way she on the one hand can wrongly maintain her 5,000 usage limit and keep updating it with new bond numbers. In order to avoid this, a free user is not allowed to edit bond number if her usage limit is already reached. Rest of the field like collection, prefix etc. can be edited though.
      NOTE: This feature is still to be implemented.
